Evolution of a Mars Car

by Jason Hoogland
Marsupial Chief Designer June - November 2001

Design Diary

Witness below evolution of the Human Operations Prototype (HOP), the first Marsupial vehicle.

In mid 2000 I prepared an initial Marsupial design, Wombat (1.4 Mb pdf).

The MSRI encouraged a simpler, cheaper and faster approach.  A small team was assembled at the University of Queensland and adapted the Wombat proposal.  The intention was to build support for Wombat, and the HOP concept proposal was based on use of second hand chassis and a cabin shaped like that of Wombat.  I identified the Mitsuibishi L300 Starwagon 4 Wheel Drive (in some countries called the Delica) as the most desirable chassis platform for cost, second hand availability and most importantly, engine location beneath the twin front seats. 

 

Base HOP vehicle - Mitsubishi L300 Starwagon 4WD Conceptual modifications Final form with Wombat-like cabin

The purchase of a basic chassis platform (HOP-B) followed MSRI support of US$5,000.  The HOP team was also provided with a donated 2 Wheel Drive Starwagon (HOP-A) , and used the next few months to dismantle both vehicles.

 

HOP-B, Starwagon 4WD, intact HOP-A, Starwagon 2WD, stripped
Removing HOP-A cabin HOP-A chassis
HOP-B with aft cabin removed HOP-B chassis

Significant differences were observed between the HOP-B and HOP-A chassis, and only the former could be used as a platform.  Members of the HOP team who contributed to this effort included Ben Cairns, Brett Robertson, Ian Clough, David Hartley and Llewellyn Mann.

I formally took on the chief designer role in April 2001.

This became an opportunity to revisit the initial concept.  Having undertaken a review of the design literature for lunar and Mars rovers, and with greater consideration given to user requirements for a real Mars rover, I began with a clean slate, removing any artificial links with Wombat.  The result was HOP-Wagon and HOP-Utility.

Tue 2 August 2001
HOP-U ver0A

This was the first distributed release of the design.  HOP-U design was progressed at the expense of HOP-W detailing.  I favour HOP-U as the first Marsupial vehicle for a number of reasons:

  • it is multi-functional, with the ability to carry an ATV and incorporate a novel inflatable,

  • it may be less expensive to build than HOP-W,

  • it offers the smallest pressurised cabin,

  • use of a tray top utility appears to be unique in the design literature,

  • while on Mars I'd want a "working vehicle" that could serve a number of functions - the utility can provide this.

Part of the rationale for Marsupial is to explore issues to assist future designers of real Mars rovers.  An important question is: how small can we make the pressurised cabin for productive sorties?  This question is a fundamental driver for the HOP.
